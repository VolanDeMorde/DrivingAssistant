Essential Rules of the Road

Key things you must know before driving in Cyprus.

Drive on the Left

Unlike most of Europe, vehicles in Cyprus drive on the left-hand side of the road and overtake on the right. This is a legacy of British rule. Ensure you look right, then left, then right again when emerging at junctions.

Zero Distractions

It is strictly prohibited to use a mobile phone (unless hands-free). Furthermore, eating and drinking (even water) while driving is illegal. You must have both hands on the wheel at all times.

Roundabout Rules

Traffic already on the roundabout has priority. Because you drive on the left, you approach clockwise and give way to traffic approaching from your right.

Speed Limits

  • Built-up areas: 50 km/h
  • National roads: 80 km/h
  • Motorways: Max 100 km/h, Min 65 km/h

Alcohol Limits

The legal limit is 0.22 micrograms of alcohol per 100 milliliters of breath (approx 0.05% BAC). For novice drivers, commercial drivers, and motorcyclists, the limit is drastically lower at 0.09 micrograms.

Seatbelts & Children

Seatbelts are mandatory for all passengers, front and rear. Children under 5 cannot travel in the front seat. Children aged 5 to 12 must use an appropriate child restraint system.
